Unit 6 : Winter X Games

0 / 0

For fans of winter sports, nothing can match the excitement of the Winter X Games. Competitors use skis, snowboards, and snowmobiles to speed down icy slopes and launch into the sky. The events include so many gravity-defying tricks that some competitors spend just as much time in the air as they do on the snow.

One exciting event is the Superpipe, which is like a half-pipe, but has much taller walls that allow snowboarders to get higher on their jumps. The athletes in the snowmobile event ─ Snocross ─ also spend a good amount of time in the air. This race involves speeds of over 95 km/h and jumps as long as 40 meters. Just watching the event gets your heart beating fast.

The X Games were started as a summer event in 1995 by ESPN, the American sports broadcaster. After a huge success, the winter games were added in 1997. Every year since then, the top winter athletes have come together to put on a great show. We should expect nothing less from the 17th Winter X Games in January 2013.

The 2013 Winter X Games will be held from January 24th to 27th in Aspen, Colorado. The games are returning to this famous ski resort community in the mountains for the 12th year in a row. Winters here are very cold, with temperature as low as -14°C and tons of snow, but this is a paradise for winter sports. More than 200 winter athletes will come with the hopes of winning a gold medal.

Gaining more fans every year, the Winter X Games had more than 100,000 people come to witness the competitions with their own eyes in 2012. Visitors can ski during the day and attend concerts at night. Best of all, they get to watch up close the amazing X Games participants who put it all on the line.

Even if you can't make it to Aspen, the games are fantastic for television audiences, as cameras follow every twist and turn of the daring athletes. While these stunts can be extremely dangerous, they're safe to watch from the sofa. Pay attention to your local TV schedule so you can catch all the action of the 2013 Winter X Games.
